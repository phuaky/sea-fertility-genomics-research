# PGT-P Primer — Polygenic Embryo Screening

## What is PGT-P?

**Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Polygenic conditions (PGT-P)** is a form of embryo screening that uses polygenic risk scores (PRS) to estimate an embryo's lifetime risk for common diseases. Unlike PGT-M (which tests for single-gene disorders like cystic fibrosis) or PGT-A (which checks chromosome count), PGT-P aggregates the effects of hundreds to thousands of genetic variants to predict risk for complex diseases.

## How it works

1. Standard IVF cycle — ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval, fertilization, embryo culture to blastocyst (day 5)
2. Trophectoderm biopsy — same biopsy taken for standard PGT-A (5-10 cells from the outer layer)
3. DNA extracted and genotyped or sequenced
4. Polygenic risk scores calculated for multiple diseases
5. Results returned to clinic (~14 days) with per-embryo risk profiles
6. Embryo selection informed by combined PGT-A + PGT-P results

## What diseases can be screened?

Varies by provider, but typically includes:

- Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes
- Coronary artery disease / heart disease
- Breast cancer
- Prostate cancer
- Schizophrenia
- Inflammatory bowel disease
- Alzheimer's disease
- Atrial fibrillation
- Obesity risk
- Low cognitive ability (framed as medical screening, not IQ selection)

## Key claims and evidence

- Individuals in the top few percent of polygenic risk may be **5-10x more likely** to develop a disease than the population average
- For height, DNA-based prediction is accurate to within ~3 cm
- Validated using sibling pair datasets (comparing predicted vs. actual outcomes within families)

- Born **May 2020**
- Parents selected from 4 embryos based on lowest combined risk for heart disease, diabetes, and cancer

- Used donor eggs with Genomic Prediction's screening

## Known limitations

### Ancestry bias
- Most polygenic risk score data comes from **European Caucasian ancestry** populations
- Predictive accuracy is significantly reduced for East Asian, South Asian, African, and other ancestries
- Available screening conditions may be more limited for non-European backgrounds
- This is the single biggest limitation for patients in Southeast Asia

### Predictive accuracy
- PRS explain only a fraction of total disease risk (environment, lifestyle, and unmeasured genetics matter too)
- Scores are population-level risk estimates, not individual-level certainties
- A "low risk" embryo can still develop disease; a "high risk" embryo may never develop it

### Ethical concerns
- European Society of Human Genetics (ESHG) has called PGT-P "unproven" and "unethical"
- Genomic Prediction's team formally rebutted the ESHG statement
- Critics raise concerns about: discarding viable embryos based on probabilistic scores, slippery slope toward trait selection, equity and access issues
- Company explicitly does not offer IQ selection, though Hsu has discussed its technical feasibility

### Regulatory landscape
- **USA:** Essentially unregulated — offered commercially
- **Singapore:** Not approved (PGT-A itself is restricted to pilot trials)
- **Thailand:** Not available (PGT-A and PGT-M are available)
- **Most of EU:** Not routinely offered
- **Australia/NZ:** Tightly regulated
