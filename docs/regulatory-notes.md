# Regulatory Notes — PGT & Embryo Screening by Country

Status of preimplantation genetic testing regulations in countries within scope. This is patient-compiled research, not legal advice.

**Priority countries:** Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore (deep coverage). Thailand, Indonesia, Brunei (maintained for reference).

**Last updated:** 2026-02-27

## Vietnam

**Regulator:** Ministry of Health (Bo Y Te) — Department of Medical Examination and Treatment Administration

### Governing law

| Instrument | Date | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Law on Marriage and Family 2014 (Law No. 52/2014/QH13) | 2014 | Top-level ART framework (Articles 93-98) — covers surrogacy, gamete donation, ART eligibility |
| Decree No. 207/2025/ND-CP | Effective 1 Oct 2025 | **Current governing decree** — replaced Decree 10/2015. Regulates donation, use, storage of sperm/eggs/embryos; ART facility conditions; altruistic surrogacy |
| Circular No. 57/2015/TT-BYT | 2015 | MOH implementation details for IVF and surrogacy conditions |

### PGT status

| Test | Status |
|---|---|
| PGT-M (monogenic disorders) | ✅ Available — published clinical research confirms use for beta-thalassaemia and other conditions at major centres |
| PGT-A (aneuploidy) | ✅ Available — routinely offered at leading clinics in Hanoi and HCMC |
| PGT-SR (structural rearrangements) | ⚠️ Likely available at advanced centres — no explicit regulatory mention found |
| PGT-P (polygenic) | ❌ Not available — no evidence of offering or regulation |
| Sex selection | ❌ Prohibited — Population Ordinance 2003 and Decree 176/2013/ND-CP ban non-medical sex identification/selection. Fines up to VND 30 million (MOH has proposed increasing to VND 100 million) |

**Regulatory note:** Vietnamese ART decrees do not explicitly name PGT subtypes. PGT falls under general ART services at licensed facilities. Vinmec advertises "3 types of preimplantation genetic testing" on their site.

### Cross-border biopsy shipping

- **Decree No. 89/2018/ND-CP** requires medical declaration (Form No. 13) for all biological samples transported across borders, submitted to border health quarantine authorities or the National Single Window Portal.
- International shipping must also comply with IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (Category B, UN3373).
- **Grey area:** No specific regulation found explicitly addressing export of embryo biopsy material for overseas PGT analysis. May require MOH approval on a case-by-case basis.
- **No Vietnamese clinic was found publicly advertising biopsy shipping to overseas labs** — requires direct clinic inquiry.

### IVF costs

| Component | VND | USD (approx.) |
|---|---|---|
| Full IVF cycle (typical range) | 60-150 million | $2,400-$6,000 |
| Embryo biopsy (for PGT) | ~8 million + 9 million/embryo analysis | ~$320 + $360/embryo |

Success rates at top centres: 50-65% per cycle. Roughly 1/5 to 1/8 of US costs.

### Recent changes (2024-2026)

**Decree 207/2025/ND-CP (effective Oct 2025)** — most significant ART overhaul in a decade:
- Single women can now access ART (egg freezing, IVF) by personal choice without infertility diagnosis
- Part of pro-natalist policy shift after Vietnam ended its two-child policy (late 2024) amid below-replacement fertility rates
- Surrogacy framework updated and clarified
- Foreigners married to Vietnamese nationals can receive donated eggs

## Malaysia

**Regulator:** Malaysian Medical Council (MMC) under the Medical Act 1971; Ministry of Health (facility standards)

### Governing law

**Malaysia has no specific ART statute.** The regulatory framework is guideline-based, not legislation-based.

| Instrument | Date | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Guideline of the MMC on Medically Assisted Reproduction | Originally MMC 003/2006; updated version uploaded 2025 | **Core governing document** — professional practice guideline (not legislation) |
| MOH Standards for ART Facility | — | Technical/operational standards for ART labs and operating theatres |
| Medical Act 1971 [Act 50] | 1971, amended 2024 (Act A1729) | General medical practice statute; gives MMC authority to issue guidelines |

**Critical caveat:** The MOH proposed an "Assisted Reproductive Technology Technique Services Act" in 2009 and again in 2016, but it was **never enacted**. MMC guidelines carry ethical/disciplinary weight but are not legally binding statutes.

### PGT status

| Test | Status |
|---|---|
| PGT-A (aneuploidy) | ✅ Permitted when clinically indicated — broadly offered at major clinics |
| PGT-M (monogenic disorders) | ✅ Permitted when clinically indicated — available at Alpha IVF, Sunfert, TMC |
| PGT-SR (structural rearrangements) | ✅ Likely permitted — falls under "clinically indicated" PGT |
| PGT-P (polygenic) | ❓ Status unclear — no clinics offer it; academic concerns raised under Islamic bioethics |
| Sex selection | ❌ Not permitted for non-medical reasons — permitted only for sex-linked genetic disorders on a case-by-case basis |

Key MMC provision: **"All PGT should only be done if it is clinically indicated."** Use of PGT for specific physical, social, or gender characteristics is restricted.

### Islamic considerations

These meaningfully influence both MMC guidelines and clinical practice in Muslim-majority Malaysia:

- **IVF permissible** provided gametes are from the married couple (5th Muzakarah 1982, 6th Muzakarah 1983). Third-party gametes are haram.
- **PGT for disease prevention** broadly welcomed — consistent with *hifz al-nafs* (protection of life) and *maslahah* (public interest).
- **Sex selection** via PGT only for sex-linked disorders, not social reasons (Islamic Fiqh Academy).
- **PGT-P** for non-disease traits (intelligence, etc.) may conflict with Islamic principles against tampering with creation (academic concern from IIUM-affiliated researchers).

### Cross-border biopsy shipping

- **No specific regulation found** governing export of embryo biopsies.
- In practice, Malaysian IVF clinics **do ship biopsies to overseas labs** (e.g., Igenomix) for PGT-A analysis — suggesting no outright prohibition exists.
- Shipping cost: ~USD 300 per shipment to Igenomix, results in ~7 working days.
- Must comply with IATA Dangerous Goods Regulations (UN3373).
- **This is the strongest indicator for our project:** if clinics already ship to Igenomix, adding Genomic Prediction as another destination is operationally straightforward.

### Recent changes (2024-2026)

- **Updated MMC Guideline (2025):** Updated from the original 2006 guideline. Full content of changes not publicly summarised.
- **Medical (Amendment) Act 2024 [Act A1729]** (effective 1 Jul 2025): Strengthens MMC regulatory framework (not ART-specific).
- **No new ART-specific legislation enacted.** The long-discussed ART Act remains unlegislated as of Feb 2026.

## Singapore

**Regulator:** Ministry of Health (MOH) via Healthcare Services Act 2020 (HCSA) licensing framework

### Governing law

| Instrument | Date | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Healthcare Services Act 2020 (HCSA) | 2020 | Primary statute — ART is one of 16 licensable healthcare services |
| Healthcare Services (Assisted Reproduction Service) Regulations 2023 | Effective 26 Jun 2023 | **Current subsidiary legislation** governing AR services under HCSA |
| Status of Children (Assisted Reproduction Technology) Act 2013 | 2013 | Legal parentage of ART-conceived children |
| Human Cloning and Other Prohibited Practices Act 2004 | 2004 | Prohibits reproductive cloning |
| Human Biomedical Research Act 2015 (HBRA) | 2015 | Governs research involving human tissue/embryos, tissue banking |

**Key MOH Circulars:**
- **Circular 95/2023** (27 Dec 2023) — Clarification on ARS Regulations and artificial insemination; sex selection prohibition
- **Circular 10/2025** (14 Mar 2025) — Written Direction for PGT Licence Conditions for CLS and ARS licensees

The older "MOH Directive 2/2005" has been **superseded** by the HCSA framework.

### PGT status

| Test | Status | Details |
|---|---|---|
| PGT-M (monogenic) | ✅ Approved mainstream since 4 May 2021 | Pilot since 2005. MOH-approved centres only, restricted to specified conditions list (Tables 2/3 in licensing terms). Includes: beta-thalassaemia, BRCA1/2, Huntington's, DMD, SMA, cystic fibrosis, haemophilia, fragile X. |
| PGT-SR (structural) | ✅ Approved mainstream since 4 May 2021 | Same announcement as PGT-M, same approval process |
| PGT-A (aneuploidy) | ⚠️ NOT approved — pilot trial only | Government-sponsored pilot at public hospitals. As of Jan 2024: 590 enrolled, 195 tested, 56 live births. Results described as "inconclusive" with "high attrition." Eligibility: age ≥35, or 2+ implantation failures, or 2+ miscarriages. MOH recommends more recruitment before mainstream approval decision. |
| PGT-P (polygenic) | ❌ Not approved | Active academic discussion (Chew et al. 2024 in *Reproductive BioMedicine Online*) but no regulatory pathway. |
| Sex selection | ❌ Prohibited except for sex-linked genetic disorders | Clarified in Circular 95/2023 |

**Embryo transfer limit:** Max 2 embryos per cycle.

### Government co-funding

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh ART cycle | Up to $7,700 (approx. 75% of costs) |
| Frozen ART cycle | Up to $2,200 |
| Cycle limits | Max 3 fresh + 3 frozen (6 total) |
| PGT-M/SR co-funding | Up to 75%, capped at SGD 17,100 (common) or SGD 31,420 (rare) per cycle, up to 6 cycles |
| MediSave | 1st: $6,000; 2nd: $5,000; 3rd+: $4,000. Lifetime cap: $15,000. Can use spouse's account. |
| Age eligibility | Below 40: full. 40+: 2 of 6 cycles, if attempted before 40. Age cap of 45 for treatment itself has been removed. |

Eligibility: legally married couples, Singapore Citizens or PRs, MOH-approved centres.

### Cross-border biopsy shipping

- **HBRA 2015** governs human tissue broadly — prohibits commercial trading, regulates tissue banks.
- **Biological Agents and Toxins Act (BATA)** covers import/transhipment of biological agents (primarily pathogens, not embryo biopsies per se).
- MOH Biosafety Branch and Singapore Customs handle biological material clearance via TradeNet.
- **Key gap:** No specific, publicly available regulation found addressing export of embryo biopsy material for offshore PGT. This may sit within AR licensing conditions (which could restrict where testing is performed) rather than customs/export law.
- **Open question from current notes remains:** Can a Singapore clinic legally ship embryo biopsy material overseas for testing not approved domestically? — TBD, needs legal/regulatory confirmation.

### Licensed AR centres

**Public (3):** NUH, SGH (Centre for Assisted Reproduction / CARE), KKH

**Private:** Multiple licensed under HCSA — includes Thomson Medical, Alpha IVF Singapore, Monash IVF Singapore, Virtus Fertility, others. No single public registry page found listing all licensed private AR centres.

### Recent changes (2024-2026)

- **HCSA Phase 2 (Jun 2023):** ART moved from old PHMCA licensing to HCSA framework
- **PGT-A pilot update (Jan 2024):** Reported to Parliament — inconclusive results, continue recruiting
- **PGT licensing update (Feb 2024):** Proposed modifications to PGT-M/SR licence conditions
- **Age cap removed:** MOH removed 45-year age limit for ART treatment; also removed cycle count cap
- **Circular 10/2025 (Mar 2025):** Formalised updated PGT licensing requirements

## Cross-border biopsy shipping (general)

**Key consideration for the PGT-P pathway:** The biopsy sample needs to be shipped from the IVF clinic to Genomic Prediction's lab in New Jersey, USA.

### What needs to happen
1. Trophectoderm biopsy taken at local clinic
2. Sample placed in transport medium / tube
3. Shipped via medical courier (e.g., World Courier, FedEx Custom Critical) in appropriate conditions
4. Received and processed at Genomic Prediction lab
5. Results returned electronically (~14 days)

### Regulatory status by priority country

| Country | Can clinics ship biopsies overseas? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Vietnam** | Unclear — Decree 89/2018 requires medical declaration for cross-border biological samples. No specific embryo biopsy regulation found. | Grey area — needs direct clinic inquiry |
| **Malaysia** | **Yes, in practice** — clinics already ship to Igenomix (~$300/shipment, 7 working days). No outright prohibition found. | Strongest pathway for Genomic Prediction partnership |
| **Singapore** | Unclear — HBRA governs tissue broadly, but no specific embryo biopsy export regulation found. May be restricted by AR licensing conditions. | Needs legal/regulatory confirmation |
| **Thailand** | Likely yes — clinics widely ship to reference labs for PGT-A | Strong existing infrastructure |

### Clinics most likely to already ship overseas
Clinics that offer PGT-A but don't have in-house genetics labs likely already ship to:
- **Igenomix** (Spain/global)
- **CooperGenomics** (USA)
- **Natera** (USA)
- **Invitae** (USA)

These clinics would have the easiest path to adding Genomic Prediction as another reference lab.
